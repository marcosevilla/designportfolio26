### Bio

I'm a Product Designer, born and raised in the Bay Area.

At Canary Technologies I lead design for our Food & Beverage, Upsells, Guest Experience, and Tipping products. The software runs inside Marriott, Wyndham, Best Western, IHG, and most of the large hotel groups you'd recognize.

Before Canary I helped democratize wine discovery at [Vivino](https://www.vivino.com/), made animated video production more accessible at [Vyond](https://www.vyond.com/), and built an all-in-one productivity tool for software engineers at [General Task](https://www.generaltask.com/).

I'm fascinated by the interplay of culture, our environment, and the tools we create to augment the way we work and create. tools: the people who use them, how culture is shaped by them, as well as our environments. I care about meticulous visual craft and deeply understanding the people we're building for and environments they reside in.  the best tools are built by those willing to look at a problem at a micro and macro scale, are grounded in culture, and  and ... shape culture, and 

The work I'm drawn to sits between three things: tools, the people using them, and the environments they show up in. Visual craft matters to me. So does paying attention to the culture the work lands inside of. What it's competing with for someone's attention. What it accidentally encourages.

I came to it the long way. As a kid I lived on PCs my dad custom-built. Maps in sim games. Edits in whatever video software I could get my hands on. Outside of design I'm shooting [concert and street photography](photo-stack), on creative side projects, singing, or on the pickleball court. Ex-tennis player. Never fully let go.

### Three guiding principles

**1. Find the simple shape under the mess.**
Reduce apparent complexity to the load-bearing invariant. F&B: every hotel ordering workflow boiled down to "where does the food go?" Compendium: one hub pattern absorbed 82% of property use cases without per-use-case dev. Most designers add structure on top of complexity. The harder move is finding the structure that was already there.

**2. Build the thing instead of pitching the thing.**
Prove ideas by making them functional, not by selling them in Figma. Code prototypes for F&B and Upsells. The AI-workflow page itself. The way I actually work day-to-day. Show beats tell.

**3. Design for the whole stay, not a single screen.**
Zoom out to the system before zooming into the surface. Compendium as the hub that connected every other guest touchpoint, not another standalone product. F&B's no-POS, modifier-aware model. The Canary design system. The screen is downstream of the architecture.

### Alternates (swap-in candidates if any of the three don't land)

- **Sweat the details no one else will.** — craft, typography, animation values. Honest, maybe too obvious for a designer.
- **The tool should disappear.** — calm software, ease as a primary value. Aligned with the north star but a little expected.
- **Bring the people you work with along.** — collaboration, the Becca modifier debate, eng partnerships. Feels less personal than the three above.
